Activation and Configuration of BitLocker in Microsoft Windows

BitLocker is a tool that's included with Microsoft Windows to enable drive encryption. To use BitLocker requires a functional called TPM module in your computer. The following tutorial will guide you through the steps required to enable BitLocker on Windows devices.

Note: BitLocker is available on machine running Windows Vista or 7 Ultimate, Windows Vista or 7 Enterprise, Windows 8.1 Pro, Windows 8.1 Enterprise, or Windows 10 Pro.

Enable BitLocker

Note: You must be logged in as an administrator.

Step 1:Go to Control Panel, and click “BitLocker Drive Encryption”.



Step 2:Now click on “Turn on BitLocker” button on right side of the respective drive which you want to encrypt.



Step 3: BitLocker will run a system check to make sure your TPM has been enabled. Wait for this check to complete. After some time, a popup window will open as shown below. First select “Use a password to unlock the drive” now you are required to specify a password for encrypting the drive, enter the password and click "Next". You should choose a password having a combination of upper & lower-case letters, numbers, spaces, and special symbols. Once the encryption is complete, you can access the drive using this password.



Step 4: Choose the appropriate option to save your recovery key – save to a file, USB flash drive, or printing the key is the simplest method. Click next after you have produced your backup key. Please note that anyone can use the recovery key to gain access to the drive, even if they do not know the password entered in the previous step, so please do not disclose it to others.



Step 5: For saving the recovery key as a file on local drive select “Save to a file” and save the key to a secure location of your local drive.



Step 6: Select as per your requirement and Click next.



Step 7: Select Encryption mode as per your requirement and Click next.



Step 8: Click on “Start encrypting” to prepare your drive for BitLocker.



Step 9: The drive encryption process will start. Encryption process could take a long time to finish depending on the size of the drive, so please be patient to wait and when completed click on close.



Step 10: Now to fully activate BitLocker you have to restart your computer. Click the button to restart your computer and then login normally after your computer restarts.After restart your drives protected with BitLocker are identified with a lock icon in Windows Explorer as shown below in fig. Double click on the locked drive to open it.



Step 11: Enter the drive password.



Step 12: Now drive is fully accessible.

Note: You need to enter your BitLocker drive Password at every startup.
